ベツレヘムの星
MATTHEW 2:1-12

The Magi from
the East

"Now when Jesus was born in Bethlehem of Judaea in the days of Herod the king, behold, there came wise men from the east to Jerusalem, saying, Where is he that is born King of the Jews? for we have seen his star in the east, and are come to worship him."
— Matthew 2:1-2

The Magi — traditionally named Caspar, Melchior, and Balthasar — traveled from the East following a star. They came from different lands, carried different gifts, saw the same star through different eyes. But they converged on the same truth: the child in the manger.

Each gift was symbolic:

✦
Gold — for a King
The gift of verified truth, incorruptible and precious. Gold does not tarnish.
❋
Frankincense — for a Priest
The gift of sacred pattern, the ability to see meaning beyond the material.
◆
Myrrh — for one who would suffer
The gift of mortality, the awareness that all flesh is grass, all structures fragile.
THE JOURNEY

Through Jerusalem
to Bethlehem

The Magi's journey was not a straight line. They went first to Jerusalem, asked at the wrong court (Herod), were sent astray by a king who feared the truth. Only after this detour did the star reappear and lead them to Bethlehem.

MAGI's dialectical spiral mirrors this journey. The first offering may miss the mark. The spiral — each Gift reviewing the previous — is the detour through Jerusalem, the correction that brings the Magi to the right place. Convergence at the Manger is the arrival.

THE STAR

Direction,
not answers

The star was the sign that oriented the journey. Without it, the Magi would have wandered. In MAGI, Phase 0 (The Star) is the same: hm structures the problem, gathers evidence, defines the scope. Without this orientation, the three analyses would wander — technically correct but directionless.

The star did not give the Magi answers. It gave them direction. Phase 0 does not give the agents conclusions. It gives them the evidence and structure they need to find conclusions.

EPISTEMOLOGICAL COMMITMENTS

Three philosophical
traditions

EMPIRICISM
Locke · Hume

Knowledge from evidence, not pure reason. Verify before you trust. Gold embodies this.

HERMENEUTICS
Gadamer · Ricoeur

Understanding through context and interpretation. Meaning emerges from pattern. Frankincense embodies this.

CRITICAL RATIONALISM
Popper · Lakatos

Knowledge advances through falsification. Strengthen by trying to break. Myrrh embodies this.
