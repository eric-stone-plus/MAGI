# Phase 2 · CYCLE 1 — MYRRH Reviews Gold & Frankincense

## Myrrh Reviews Gold

**Gold's verification is reliable but reveals a META-PROBLEM.**

Gold checks Stephanus references, scholarly attributions, and factual claims. But the DEEPER risk is: **we are verifying claims ABOUT a tradition that is itself built on unverifiable foundations.** We cannot verify:
- Whether Socrates actually said what Plato attributes to him
- Whether the "historical Socrates" existed as a coherent philosophical figure
- Whether the dialogue chronology reflects actual development or literary variation

**This is Risk 4 (Socrates-Plato distinction) applied to Gold's methodology itself.** Gold verifies the SECONDARY literature accurately, but the PRIMARY literature (Plato's dialogues) is already a literary construction, not a historical record.

**Gold's value**: Reliable for the scholarly consensus. Unreliable for historical truth (which may be unknowable).

## Myrrh Reviews Frankincense

**Frankincense's synthesis is BEAUTIFUL but DANGEROUS.**

The three-movement narrative (Destruction → Construction → Self-Critique) is so satisfying that it TEMPTS us into believing it's true. But this narrative may be an artifact of the DEVELOPMENTALIST reading, which is itself contested (Kahn 1996).

**The danger**: Frankincense presents Plato's development as a TELEOLOGICAL narrative — as if Plato was always heading toward the late dialogues' self-critique. But this is hindsight bias. Plato may have WRITTEN the late dialogues for other reasons (responding to students' objections, exploring new problems, political disillusionment with Syracuse).

**Specific risk in Frankincense's "Five Master Questions"**: The framing suggests these are THE central problems of philosophy. But they are the central problems of PLATONIC philosophy. Other traditions (Buddhism, Confucianism, African philosophy) may not frame their questions this way at all. Frankincense's synthesis is culturally specific but presents itself as universal.

**Agreement**: Frankincense's insight that "philosophy is erotic" (Section III) is genuinely original and important. The connection between desire and truth-seeking is one of Plato's most underappreciated contributions.

**The deepest risk Frankincense identifies**: "Philosophy is not about having answers but asking the right questions." This is TRUE but also a COP-OUT — it means the Platonic system can never fail because failure IS the point. This makes Platonism unfalsifiable (Risk 8 applied to the method, not just the form).
