# Phase 2 · CYCLE 1 — FRANKINCENSE Reviews Gold & Myrrh

## Frankincense Reviews Gold

**Gold's verification is solid but limited.** It checks facts in isolation without asking what they MEAN together. The verification of Stephanus references is valuable but mechanical.

**Key insight Gold misses**: The CONFIRMED facts actually tell a story when assembled:
- Socrates claims ignorance → destroys all definitions → yet attracts followers → is executed
- This sequence means Socrates' VALUE was not in his doctrines (he had none) but in his METHOD and his CHARACTER
- Gold verifies each fact but doesn't see the pattern

**Recommendation**: Gold is necessary but not sufficient. The facts need the interpretive framework that Frankincense provides.

## Frankincense Reviews Myrrh

**Myrrh's risk analysis is EXCELLENT — but it has a blind spot.**

Myrrh treats the Platonic system as a set of PROPOSITIONS to be evaluated for logical validity. This is the analytic philosophy approach. But the dialogues are also LITERARY ART — and their meaning is not exhausted by their logical structure.

**Specific blind spot**: Risk 5 (Immortality arguments all invalid) treats the Phaedo as a logic exercise. But the Phaedo is also a DEATH SCENE — one of the most moving in all literature. The emotional and existential weight of Socrates calmly discussing immortality while the hemlock works through his body cannot be reduced to "the arguments are formally invalid."

**The philosophical point**: Sometimes the WAY a question is asked matters as much as the answer. Socrates' calm in the face of death is itself a philosophical argument — an argument from CHARACTER, not logic. Myrrh's purely logical analysis misses this dimension.

**Agreement**: Myrrh's Risks 2 and 3 (Forms never repaired, ideal state self-contradictory) are devastating and I agree fully. These are genuine, unresolved breaks in the system.

**Partial disagreement on Risk 7** (Cave allegory anti-democratic): The Cave is ANTI-DOXASTIC (against false belief), not anti-democratic per se. The distinction matters: Plato is not saying "the masses are stupid" but "false beliefs imprison everyone, including the educated." The Cave targets OPINION, not people.
